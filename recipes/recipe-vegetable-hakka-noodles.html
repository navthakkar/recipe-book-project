vegetable hakka noodles

serving size: serves 2

recipe origin: mainland china cook with manali

tags: vegan entrée indo-chinese

ingredients

  • 300 g noodles (ching hakka noodles!)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons minced ginger
  • 1 green chili sliced
  • 1 stalk celery chopped
  • 1 medium red onion sliced
  • 1 large carrot sliced
  • 1 large red pepper sliced
  • 3 stalks green onion chopped
  • 2.5 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar (or white vinegar)
  • 1 tsp hot sauce (sriracha)
  • ½ tsp sugar (optional)
  • ¼ tsp black pepper powder
  • a pinch white pepper powder
  • 1 tsp chili oil

instructions

  1. chop all the veggies before you start making the noodles
  2. boil the noodles according to instructions on the package
  3. drain the noodles & wash under cold running water, this stops the cooking process. add 1/2 tablespoon of vegetable oil to the noodles & toss so that the noodles get lightly coated with the oil & don’t stick to each other. set it aside
  4. heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil & 1 tablespoon sesame oil on medium-high heat. once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic & ginger, sliced green chili & chopped celery
  5. sauté for few seconds until the ginger garlic starts to change color
  6. add sliced onions & sauté for a minute or two until the sides of the onion starts turning light golden brown
  7. add in the sliced carrots, bell pepper, green onion & cook for 1 minute on high heat. the veggies should remain crunchy
  8. push veggies to the side, lower the heat & add soy sauce, rice vinegar, hot sauce (like sriracha) & sugar (if using)
  9. toss the veggies to combine well with the sauce. add in the black pepper, salt & white pepper
  10. stir in the boiled noodles. using a pair of tongs, mix it well so that the noodles are well coated with the sauce
  11. as a final touch, stir in a teaspoon of chili oil
  12. toss the noodles well & garnish with more spring onion greens
  13. serve hakka noodles as such or with your favorite indo-chinese dishes!
