vegan tonkotsu ramen

serving size: serves 2

recipe origin: bon appétit

tags: vegan japanese ramen entrée

ingredients

  • 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1/8 cup plus 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • ½ tbsp black or white sesame seeds
  • ½ tbsp gochugaru (coarse korean red pepper powder) or 1 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
  • kosher salt
  • 2 scallions
  • 1” piece ginger, peeled, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 4 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 2x2” piece dried kombu
  • 1 ½ tbsp unsalted butter, cut in pieces
  • ½ tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 baby bok choy, quartered lengthwise
  • 2 5-oz packages fresh ramen noodles
  • jammy eggs
  • toasted nori sheets
  • cilantro for garnishing

instructions

  1. cook garlic & 1/8 cup oil in a medium pot over medium heat, stirring often, until garlic is beginning to turn golden, about 2 mins
  2. stir in sesame seeds & cook, stirring occasionally, until garlic is golden brown & crisp, about 1 min. transfer mixture to a small bowl & stir in gochugaru; season w/ salt. set garlic oil aside. wipe out pot & set aside
  3. trim dark green parts from scallions & thinly slice; set aside for serving. Coarsely chop white & pale green parts
  4. heat 2 tbsp oil in a reserved pot over medium-high. cook chopped scallions & ginger, stirring often, until scallions are charred in spots, about 4 mins
  5. add tomato paste & cook, stirring occasionally, until it begins to stick to the bottom of the pot & darkens slightly, about 2 mins. add mushrooms & kombu, then stir in 2 cups vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then remove from heat & let sit until mushrooms soften, about 10 mins. remove & discard kombu
  6. using a slotted spoon, transfer solids to a blender. Add a ladleful or 2 of broth to the blender & purée until smooth. stir purée back into broth in pot & bring to a simmer over medium heat
  7. add butter a piece at a time, whisking to combine after each addition before adding more. stir in soy sauce, season with salt. reduce heat to low & keep warm until ready to serve
  8. meanwhile, bring a large pot of water to a boil. add bok choy & cook until bright green & tender, about 2 mins. using slotted spoon, transfer bok choy to a plate
  9. return water to boil & add an egg to it. boil for 6 mins if you want a runny yolk, 8 for a cooked yolk. once finished, place in a water bath to stop further cooking
  10. meanwhile, cook noodles according to package instructions. drain & divide among bowls
  11. to serve, ladle broth over noodles, then top with bok choy & reserved garlic oil. top with eggs, nori & cilantro is desired
